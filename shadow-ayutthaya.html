Shadow of Ayutthaya

A 2D Stealth-Action game inspired by historical events.

Project Overview

Shadow of Ayutthaya is a 2D stealth-action game developed in Godot 4, set during a historical conflict in Southeast Asia. The player assumes the role of a commoner who, after completing his forced labor duties, infiltrates an enemy-controlled Burmese military camp to rescue his younger brother.

Gameplay focuses on stealth traversal, enemy perception management, and high-risk close-quarters encounters. The narrative context supports the gameplay by placing the player in asymmetrical, high-stakes scenarios where avoidance, timing, and situational awareness are critical.

Details & Responsibilities

  • Stealth AI System: Implemented enemy guard behavior using a state-based architecture, including transitions between Idle, Suspicious, and Alert states, driven by player visibility and sound cues.
  • Enemy Perception & Detection: Developed field-of-view (FOV) detection using raycasting combined with Area2D monitoring to simulate vision cones, line-of-sight checks, and
  • QTE Combat System: Designed and implemented timing-based Quick Time Event mechanics for silent takedowns and close-quarters encounters, reinforcing stealth-oriented gameplay.
  • Signal-Driven Architecture: Utilized Godot’s signal system to decouple gameplay logic from UI and audio systems, improving maintainability and modularity.
  • Hand-Drawn Asset Pipeline: Created original 2D sprites and animations and integrated them into the gameplay pipeline, ensuring consistency between visual feedback and underlying mechanics.
